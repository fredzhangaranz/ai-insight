# Top 20 Clinical Questions for Wound Care Clinicians

**Purpose**: Marketing reference - realistic questions clinicians ask using Silhouette + AI Insight Tool  
**Audience**: Wound care nurses, physicians, clinical leaders, quality teams  
**Use**: Demo scripts, marketing materials, conference presentations

---

## 📊 Overview by Category

The questions are organized into 6 clinical categories that reflect real wound care workflows:

1. **Treatment Effectiveness** (Questions 1-4)
2. **Patient Outcomes & Healing** (Questions 5-8)
3. **Resource Utilization & Cost** (Questions 9-11)
4. **Quality & Risk Management** (Questions 12-15)
5. **Population Health & Trends** (Questions 16-18)
6. **Operational Efficiency** (Questions 19-20)

---

## Category 1: Treatment Effectiveness

### 1. "Which dressing type shows the best healing rates for diabetic foot ulcers?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- Need evidence to validate or change treatment protocols
- Want to optimize product formulary decisions
- Support cost-benefit analysis for procurement

**What the answer reveals:**

- Comparative healing times by dressing category
- Statistical significance of differences
- Can filter by wound severity, patient characteristics

**Example visualization:** Bar chart comparing average healing time (days) for different dressing types

---

### 2. "Are compression bandages more effective than compression stockings for venous leg ulcers?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- Clinical debate about optimal compression therapy
- Patient compliance varies between modalities
- Cost differences require justification

**What the answer reveals:**

- Healing rates for each compression type
- Time to closure comparison
- Can stratify by patient age, mobility, compliance

**Example visualization:** Line chart showing healing progression over time for each modality

---

### 3. "Do pressure injuries respond better to negative pressure therapy or standard wound care?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- NPT (NPWT) is expensive - need ROI justification
- Not all wounds are candidates for NPT
- Hospital policy may require evidence

**What the answer reveals:**

- Healing rate comparison by treatment type
- Time to closure and reduction in wound size
- Cost per healed wound (if cost data available)

**Example visualization:** Comparison chart with healing time and cost metrics

---

### 4. "What's the success rate of different debridement methods for sloughy wounds?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- Multiple debridement options (sharp, enzymatic, autolytic)
- Different methods suit different clinical situations
- Training and competency considerations

**What the answer reveals:**

- Frequency of complete debridement by method
- Time to clean wound bed
- Complications or adverse events

**Example visualization:** Stacked bar chart showing success rates and time to clean wound bed

---

## Category 2: Patient Outcomes & Healing

### 5. "What's the average healing time for diabetic foot ulcers treated in our facility?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- Benchmark against national standards
- Track improvement over time
- Report to administrators and payers

**What the answer reveals:**

- Mean and median healing times
- Distribution of outcomes (fast, slow, non-healing)
- Comparison to previous periods or national benchmarks

**Example visualization:** Histogram showing distribution of healing times with average line

---

### 6. "How do wound outcomes differ between patients with good vs. poor glycemic control?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- Validate importance of diabetes management education
- Identify high-risk patients needing extra support
- Support interdisciplinary referrals (endocrinology)

**What the answer reveals:**

- Healing rates stratified by HbA1c levels or blood glucose
- Time to closure comparison
- Complication rates (infection, amputation)

**Example visualization:** Scatter plot or grouped bar chart by glycemic control categories

---

### 7. "Which patient factors predict poor wound healing outcomes?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- Identify high-risk patients early
- Tailor interventions to risk factors
- Support risk stratification models

**What the answer reveals:**

- Top predictive factors (age, comorbidities, nutrition, mobility)
- Relative impact of each factor
- Combined risk profiles

**Example visualization:** Horizontal bar chart ranking factors by predictive power

---

### 8. "Are our surgical site infection rates improving over time?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- Quality metric tracked for accreditation
- Indicator of protocol adherence
- Early warning for process breakdown

**What the answer reveals:**

- Infection rate trends by month/quarter
- Comparison to target rates or benchmarks
- Breakdown by surgery type or surgeon

**Example visualization:** Line chart with trend line and target threshold

---

## Category 3: Resource Utilization & Cost

### 9. "What's our average cost per healed wound by wound type?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- CFO wants cost efficiency metrics
- Support value-based care contracting
- Identify opportunities for cost reduction without sacrificing quality

**What the answer reveals:**

- Total treatment cost divided by successful closures
- Breakdown by dressing costs, labor, overhead
- Comparison across wound categories

**Example visualization:** Bar chart comparing cost per healed wound across categories

---

### 10. "How many dressing changes does the average pressure injury require before healing?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- Estimate resource needs (supplies, nursing time)
- Support staffing decisions
- Identify outliers requiring case review

**What the answer reveals:**

- Mean/median dressing changes by pressure injury stage
- Total days of care
- Variability (some heal fast, some very slow)

**Example visualization:** Box plot showing distribution by wound stage

---

### 11. "Which wounds consume the most nursing time per week?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- Optimize staffing and scheduling
- Identify patients needing care coordinator
- Support workload balancing across team

**What the answer reveals:**

- Ranking of wound types by time burden
- Individual patients requiring most time
- Trends over time (getting better or worse?)

**Example visualization:** Pie chart of total nursing hours by wound type

---

## Category 4: Quality & Risk Management

### 12. "What percentage of our pressure injuries are hospital-acquired vs. present on admission?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- Quality metric—hospital-acquired = preventable
- CMS penalties for hospital-acquired pressure injuries (Stage 3+)
- Identify gaps in prevention protocols

**What the answer reveals:**

- Proportion of hospital-acquired wounds
- Trends over time (improving or worsening?)
- High-risk units or patient populations

**Example visualization:** Stacked area chart showing hospital-acquired vs. POA over time

---

### 13. "Are we documenting wound assessments consistently across all shifts?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- Quality assurance and audit preparation
- Legal/malpractice risk mitigation
- Identify training needs

**What the answer reveals:**

- Completion rate of required assessment fields
- Variation by shift, unit, or individual nurse
- Gaps in documentation (photos, measurements, etc.)

**Example visualization:** Heatmap showing completion rates by shift and day

---

### 14. "What's our readmission rate for patients discharged with wounds?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- CMS penalty avoidance (30-day readmissions)
- Indicator of discharge planning quality
- Support for wound clinic or home health services

**What the answer reveals:**

- 30-day readmission rate
- Primary reasons for readmission (infection, wound deterioration)
- Patient characteristics associated with readmission

**Example visualization:** Funnel chart showing discharge → readmission pathway

---

### 15. "Which patients have wounds that aren't healing as expected?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- Proactive identification of problems
- Trigger for interdisciplinary case review
- Prevent complications (infection, amputation)

**What the answer reveals:**

- List of patients with stalled or worsening wounds
- Expected vs. actual healing trajectory
- Alerts for clinical review

**Example visualization:** Table with flags for wounds not meeting healing milestones

---

## Category 5: Population Health & Trends

### 16. "What's the prevalence of diabetic foot ulcers in our patient population?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- Understand caseload and resource needs
- Support business case for specialized diabetic foot clinic
- Track trends (increasing, stable, decreasing?)

**What the answer reveals:**

- Number and percentage of DFU patients
- Trends over time
- Geographic or demographic patterns

**Example visualization:** Line chart showing DFU prevalence over time

---

### 17. "How has our wound care quality improved since implementing the new protocol?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- Validate practice changes
- Support continued investment in new protocols
- Demonstrate quality improvement to leadership

**What the answer reveals:**

- Pre/post comparison of key metrics (healing time, infection rate, etc.)
- Statistical significance of improvement
- ROI of protocol change

**Example visualization:** Before/after bar chart or line chart with protocol implementation marker

---

### 18. "What's the infection rate for wounds by anatomical location?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- Certain sites (sacrum, foot) more infection-prone
- Inform prevention strategies
- Support targeted interventions

**What the answer reveals:**

- Infection percentage by location
- Severity of infections (local vs. systemic)
- Trends over time

**Example visualization:** Body map heatmap or bar chart by location

---

## Category 6: Operational Efficiency

### 19. "How many patients are currently being treated for pressure injuries across all facilities?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- Census for resource planning
- Workload balancing across facilities
- Capacity planning for specialized units

**What the answer reveals:**

- Current active wounds by facility
- Stage distribution (mostly Stage 2 vs. deeper wounds)
- Expected duration of care based on historical data

**Example visualization:** Dashboard with current census by facility and stage

---

### 20. "What percentage of wounds have clinical photos documented at each visit?"

**Why clinicians ask this:**

- Quality metric for complete documentation
- Telemedicine and legal protection
- Accreditation requirement (some programs)

**What the answer reveals:**

- Photo documentation compliance rate
- Variation by provider, unit, or shift
- Trends over time (improving with training?)

**Example visualization:** Gauge chart showing percentage with trend arrow

## 🎨 Visual Examples for Each Question Type

### Treatment Effectiveness Questions (1-4)

**Best visualization:** Side-by-side comparison charts

- Bar charts for categorical comparisons
- Include sample size and confidence intervals
- Color-code by performance (green = best, orange = moderate, red = concerning)

### Patient Outcomes Questions (5-8)

**Best visualization:** Trend lines and distribution charts

- Histogram showing healing time distribution
- Scatter plots for correlation analysis
- Benchmark lines for national standards

### Resource Utilization Questions (9-11)

**Best visualization:** Cost breakdowns and time analysis

- Stacked bar charts showing cost components
- Pie charts for time allocation
- Tables with drill-down capability

### Quality & Risk Questions (12-15)

**Best visualization:** Compliance metrics and alerts

- Gauge charts for percentage metrics
- Traffic light indicators (red/yellow/green)
- Alert lists with patient identifiers

### Population Health Questions (16-18)

**Best visualization:** Trend analysis and prevalence

- Line charts over time
- Before/after comparisons
- Heatmaps for geographic or demographic patterns

### Operational Questions (19-20)

**Best visualization:** Dashboards and real-time metrics

- Live census numbers
- Compliance percentages with targets
- Capacity indicators

## 📊 Question Difficulty & Platform Capabilities

### Simple Questions (Single Data Source)

**Examples:** #5, #16, #19, #20

- Single table queries
- Basic aggregations (count, average, percentage)
- **Response time:** <3 seconds
- **Skill level:** Any clinician, no training needed

### Moderate Questions (Multiple Data Sources)

**Examples:** #1, #2, #3, #6, #8, #10, #11, #18

- Join multiple tables (wounds + treatments + outcomes)
- Group by categories and compare
- **Response time:** 3-8 seconds
- **Skill level:** Still plain English, but more specific

### Complex Questions (Analysis & Prediction)

**Examples:** #4, #7, #9, #12, #13, #14, #15, #17

- Multiple data sources with time series
- Statistical analysis or risk prediction
- May require sub-question breakdown
- **Response time:** 8-15 seconds (with AI planning)
- **Skill level:** More detailed question, but still conversational

**Marketing Message:**

> "From simple census checks to complex predictive analytics—all through the same conversational interface"

## 💡 Pro Tips for Marketing

1. **Make it Interactive**: Let prospects type their own question at demos/conferences
2. **Show the Follow-up**: Don't just answer one question—show how you can immediately ask "why?" or "what about...?"
3. **Use Real Scenarios**: Ground questions in real clinical situations ("During morning rounds..." or "After discharge...")
4. **Quantify the Benefit**: "This question used to require a 3-week custom report. Now: 8 seconds."
5. **Collect More Questions**: Every demo, every conference—ask "What questions would YOU want to ask?"
